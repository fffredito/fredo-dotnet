Wil-fredo hails from the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico. With a passion for learning and visual languages, he has immersed himself in various creative fields, including filmmaking, web development, and visual arts. Wilfredo pursued his academic interests in the U.S., earning a Bachelor’s Degree in Film.

Throughout his career, he has taken on multiple roles in production, working as a cinematographer for short films such as Dynamite Abraham: A Documentary and the upcoming Sexy Bitch. He also directed and edited the music video for the single Thunder by the Boston band Pyramid Thieves. His versatility extends to roles as a producer, gaffer and sound technician.

Wilfredo's creative journey expanded after attending an introductory course on video signals, hosted by the Wavefield collective in Bushwick, New York. This experience opened his mind to analog video mixers, visual programming languages, and using web technologies as an art tool. Wilfredo experiments with a mix of digital and analog, focusing on video manipulation and generative art.

Now based in New York, he continues to produce and photograph short films and welcomes collaborative projects with fellow artists.
